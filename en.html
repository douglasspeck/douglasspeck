Research Projects

Auto-Corrective Digital Notebooks

2022/1 - 2023/2

The project consisted in the development of a digital interactive learning platform for mathematics teaching based in learning trails and inspired in the Auto-corrective Notebooks: Fractions developed by the pedagogic residency of Math Education at Unicamp. The platform was created with modular and replicable resources, with an intuitive and user-friendly interface. The goal was to provide a positive experience of learning to the users and help them understand math activities from examples and tests.

I started the project in 2022, researching more about the auto-corrective notebooks and in 2023 started the platform's development, which has been discontinued.

During this project, I was awarded a 'PICME' research grant and oriented by professor Marcelo Firer.

Monitoring Fernanda

2023/1 - 2024/1

Fernanda was an elementary school student. On account of respiratory failures during babyhood, she had compromised motor function, memory and speak (Wernicke's Syndrome). She relies on an oxygenation device, besides a permanent tracheostomy tube. Because of her limited communication, I was hired to help integrate her to the educational environment, acting as an intermediate between her and her teachers and colleagues. Thus, the process focused on Inclusion.

Fernanda studied in a private small school, with one class per grade, which fomented high interation among students, continued contact with teachers and, in general, her inclusion. School's method was traditional: student handout and chalkboard; hence, I sought to bring supplementary material to auxiliate her.

Furthermore, Fernanda is in literacy process, being monitored by a psychopedagogue and a speech therapist. As reported, there was a previous attempt to educate her in sign language, but her motor limitations disrupted the process. Since then, she had been oralized with a mixed model (which uses signs to emphasize phonemes). During my work with her, I was able to teach her over 30 signs in Brazilian Sign Language (LIBRAS), with which she could compose a few ideas.
